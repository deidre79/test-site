A Rural Prefecture's Hidden Gems

Mifuneyama mountain in April, when the green has returned but the azaleas at the base of the mountain haven't yet reached peak bloom. Source: Deidre Foley

Located on Japan's southern island of Kyushu, Saga Prefecture borders Fukuoka, Nagasaki, and the Sea of Japan. Saga is not particularly well-known among foreign tourists and even within Japan it's known as a mostly rural prefecture. However, it's known as a birthplace of ceramics, as well as for sites like Karatsu Castle, Yutoku Inari Shrine, the Yoshinogari Historical Park, and Mifuneyama Rakuen.

The largest cities in Saga Prefecture by population (from 2020) are:

  1. Saga (233,466)
  2. Karatsu (117,431)
  3. Tosu (74,258)
  4. Imari (52,651)
  5. Takeo (47,962)
